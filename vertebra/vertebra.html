The Vertebra Column

The vertebra column, also known as the Backbone or Spinal column, is the central supporting structure of the skeleton.
The vertebra column forms the backbone of vertebrate animals and protect the spinal cord.It is made up of five groups
of bones called the vertebrae (singular: vertebra) in mammals. In mammals. In human, it consist of 33 vertebrae, in it
consist of 46 vertebrae. The vertebrae are held one to the others with strong ligaments having compressible cartilage pads
intervertebral discs between consecutive vertebrae.

Cervical Vertibrae

The cervical vertebrae are found in the neck region. In man, they are seven in number.
The first cervical vertebra is called the atlas while the second is called the axis. The
third to the seventh bones are the normal cervical bones.

Characteristics of the Atlas

  • The atlas vertebra (fig. 1.4) has a large neural canal.
  • It has a flat and broad transverse process.
  • The nueral spine very short or absent.
  • The centrum is absent
  • I has a vertebrarterial canal for passage of blood vessels.

Function of the Atlas

It allows the head to nod on it (i.e it permit nodding of head) as it fit into the occipital
condyles of the skull.

Characteristics of the Axis

  • The axis (fig. 1.4(a and b)) has a broad and flat centrum.
  • It articulate with the atlas through the odontoid process.
  • It has a large flattened neural spine.
  • The transverse process is reduced to a pin-like structure.
  • It has a vertebrarterial canal.

Functions of the Axis

It allows the head to be turned easily, i.e, twisting of the head.
